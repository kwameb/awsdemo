If you've been injured in a car accident due to someone else's negligence, you likely have a case for an auto accident claim. It's best to consult with a knowledgeable attorney who can assess the details of your situation and provide you with personalized advice.

3. What damages can I recover in an auto accident case?
In an auto accident case, you may be able to recover damages for medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and property damage. An experienced attorney can help you understand the full scope of potential damages in your specific case.

4. How long do I have to file a claim after an auto accident in Alpharetta?
In Alpharetta, the statute of limitations for filing an auto accident claim is typically two years from the date of the accident. However, it's important to consult with a skilled attorney as soon as possible to ensure you don't miss any important deadlines.

5. What if the other driver doesn't have insurance?
If the other driver involved in the accident doesn't have insurance, you may still have options for recovering compensation through your own insurance policy. An attorney can help you explore these options and advocate for your rights.

6. Will I have to go to court for my auto accident case?
Many auto accident cases are settled out of court through negotiations with insurance companies. However, if a fair settlement cannot be reached, your attorney can represent you in court to seek the compensation you deserve.

7. How much does it cost to hire an auto accident attorney in Alpharetta?
Most auto accident attorneys work on a contingency fee basis, which means they only get paid if they secure a settlement or verdict for you. This allows you to pursue your case without any upfront costs.
